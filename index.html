08

Methodology notes

Generality. The engine appraises any recurring-revenue deal that fits the shape: an acquired cohort of units (subscribers, customers, users) paying a monthly price, decaying at a churn rate, against a fixed commitment, a revenue share and launch costs. Templates re-range the inputs for streaming licences, B2B subscriptions and app partnerships; term, ramp, payment schedule and currency are all configurable, and any deal can be exported to a JSON file and re-imported later.

Revenue build. Gross adds phase in front-weighted across the configurable launch ramp and the cohort decays at the monthly churn rate; revenue is cohort size × ARPU per month over the deal term, scored at contribution margin — net of payment processing, delivery and shared content costs — rather than gross revenue. Only incremental economics are scored — the base business is deliberately excluded so the deal stands on its own.

Cost build. The fixed commitment is paid at signing per the configurable upfront percentage, with the remainder split equally across annual milestones inside the term. Revenue share accrues monthly on cohort revenue. Marketing is weighted to launch; localisation & ops run monthly. The delivery cost variance lever stresses the controllable cost base only, since the MG cannot overrun.

Appraisal. NPV discounts monthly net cash flows at the annual hurdle rate converted to a monthly equivalent. IRR is solved by bisection on monthly flows and annualised. Payback is the first month cumulative undiscounted cash turns positive.

Unit economics. CAC is acquisition marketing per gross add; LTV is monthly contribution per unit net of revenue share, multiplied by expected paying months within the term, (1−(1−c)^T)/c. LTV:CAC and CAC payback are benchmarked against illustrative sector ranges. The fixed commitment is deliberately excluded from CAC — it is the cost of the asset, not of acquisition — which is why healthy unit economics can coexist with a marginal NPV.

Sensitivity. The tornado flexes one driver at a time between an adverse and favourable bound (e.g. adds ±25%, churn ±1pp, margin ±5pp) with all others held constant, ranking drivers by NPV swing. Break-evens are solved by bisection: the gross adds, churn rate and minimum guarantee at which NPV crosses zero, holding all other terms.

Simulation. Each iteration draws gross adds from a triangular distribution (skewed to under-delivery), churn from a clamped normal around the input, and cost variance from a triangular distribution skewed to overrun — reflecting the empirical asymmetry of content deals. By default the draws are correlated through a shared launch-quality factor (ρ = 0.6 between adds shortfall and cost overrun, ρ = 0.3 with churn) — a weak launch tends to miss on volume, overrun on cost and churn faster together, and independent draws would understate tail risk. The toggle allows comparison against independent sampling. 4,000 iterations produce the NPV distribution, percentiles and probability of value creation.
